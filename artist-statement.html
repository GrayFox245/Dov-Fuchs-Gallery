Artist statement

From Perception to Inner Rooms

Images are rooms of thought: thresholds, mirrors, absences, theatrical encounters, and impossible situations.

Dov Fuchs treats digital construction as a way to stage inner experience. The work often begins with photography and becomes an imagined scene where psychological states take architectural form.

The images move through self-portraits, altered realities, memory, humor, anxiety, mortality, and wonder. They do not simply illustrate ideas; they create visual situations in which the viewer enters a condition of uncertainty.

The gallery extends this approach into space. It is not only a container for images, but another layer of the work: a navigable mental architecture where series, documents, and encounters unfold gradually.
